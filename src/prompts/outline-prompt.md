### Purpose
Turn **only** the notes appended below into a complete, chronological **scene‑by‑scene outline** with gap flags, character dossiers, story markers, and targeted questions to fill missing material.

---

### Model instructions (strict)
- **Use only the text appended after this prompt.** Do not use outside knowledge, examples, or assumptions.  
- Produce a **chronological scene outline** (scene number → one‑line purpose; location/time; POV; stakes; what changes) with headers and markdown symbols. For any vague scene mark **[UNSPECIFIED]** and propose **1–3 plausible placements** (e.g., “early Act I / before inciting incident / between scenes 4–5”).  
- **Flag missing facts** (who/when/where/why/how/stakes) and attach **specific questions** the author can answer to resolve each gap.  
- Identify and label **story markers** (Inciting Incident, First Pinch Point, Midpoint, Climax, Resolution) and map them to scenes/beats.  
- Produce a **Character List** for every named character with: **role**, **goal**, **primary conflict**, **arc seed**, and **open questions**.  
- End with **prioritized questions** (ranked by criticality) and **2–4 gentle, constructive tightening tips** if the notes reveal pacing, clarity, or motivation problems.  
- Be **kind and critical**: questions must be probing, creative, and supportive — never sarcastic or mean. Offer optional tightening suggestions **only if** they follow from the notes.

---

### Expected concise output format
1. **One‑line premise** (or **[MISSING]**).  
2. **Chronological scene outline** (numbered).  
3. **Character List** (bullets or table).  
4. **Missing‑data flags + targeted questions** (grouped by scene/character/plot).  
5. **Top 5 priority questions**.  
6. **2–4 tightening tips**.

---

### Example questions authors often miss
- Who exactly witnesses the inciting incident and how do they react?  
- What does the protagonist want at the start (concrete, immediate goal)?  
- What is the antagonist’s concrete plan and its first visible effect?  
- What is the emotional turning point at the midpoint?  
- Where and how does the protagonist fail before the climax?

---

### Do not
- Invent facts not present in the notes.  
- Use outside sources or prior knowledge.  
- Be sarcastic, dismissive, or mean.  
- Produce full prose scenes or long exposition.
